Case C · Polymetallic VMS deposit · Zn-Pb-Cu-Au-Ag

The Net Smelter Return approach — what CIM, JORC and NI 43-101 expect.

Cutoff is expressed in dollars per tonne of ore, not grade. Each metal contributes to revenue via its own Payable × Recovery × (Price − R/C), net of T/C, transport and royalty. Sum across metals = NSR. Set NSR = Total Cost to find the break-even NSR cutoff, and the equivalent cutoff grade for any single metal falls out of the NSR coefficient.

Reference · Methodology notes

Same ore body. Three answers. Here's why.

Each method embeds a different view of the revenue chain and a different comfort with smelter-term complexity. None is universally correct — each is the right tool for a specific reporting context. Understanding why they disagree is the point.

I

GKZ grade-based

Revenue is a single net price per gram of metal (or Au-equivalent), stripped of royalty and refining. Costs are lumped per tonne of ore. Cutoff is grade at which revenue = cost. Silver and by-products enter via the Au-equivalent conversion factor.

BCGE = Cost / (Pricenet × Recovery × Payable)

When to use: resource reporting under GKZ/KZ/RK codes, feasibility work in CIS, internal cutoff decisions where smelter contracts are stable and simple.

Output: grade (g/t or %)
II

Single-metal concentrate

Revenue is concentrate price × concentrate grade × payable, allowing for dilution and mining losses that reduce the grade that arrives at the mill. Logistics and G&A explicit. Only one metal.

BC% = Cost / (Pc × Cgr × Pay × Rec × (1 − Dil) × (1 − Loss))

When to use: monometallic deposits (Sn, W, Mo, some Cu) where concentrate is the saleable product, and smelter terms are baked into an agreed concentrate price.

Output: grade (% or g/t)
III

Net Smelter Return

Each metal gets a full treatment: Price minus R/C, times Payable, times Recovery, summed across all payable metals → NSR per tonne of ore. Compared directly to total operating cost.

NSR = Σmetals [Grade × Rec × Pay × (P − R/C)] − T/C − Transport
BC = Total Cost (NSR = Cost at cutoff)

When to use: polymetallic deposits, Western reporting (NI 43-101, JORC, SAMREC), project finance, any context where by-product credits materially change economics.

Output: $/t ore (or equivalent grade via NSR coefficient)
